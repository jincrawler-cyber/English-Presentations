Expressing Purpose: TO vs FOR

1. Look and Compare

How do we answer the question "Why?"

Column A

  • I am going to Hawaii
    to relax.
  • I need a passport
    to travel.
  • She packed a map
    to find her way.

Column B

  • I am going to Hawaii
    for a vacation.
  • I need a passport
    for the trip.
  • She packed a map
    for directions.

Column C

  • A camera is
    for taking photos.
  • A big suitcase is
    for carrying clothes.
  • Sunglasses are
    for protecting eyes.

Read the sentences. Do they have the same meaning?

2. Let's Discover

What kind of words come after TO?
(relax, travel, find) Verbs (Base Form)
What kind of words come after FOR?
(a vacation, the trip, directions) Nouns (Things)
When explaining an object's function...
(taking, carrying, protecting) FOR + Verb(ing)

3. The Rules

A. Personal Purpose (Why I do something):

Use TO + Verb (Base form)
Use FOR + Noun (A thing)

B. Object Function (What a thing is used for):

Use FOR + Verb-ING
